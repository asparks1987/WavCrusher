Made for the shelf, not the session

Keep the masters. Reclaim the space.

WAV is dependable, but uncompressed audio eats disks quickly. WavCrusher is for collections you still value but rarely open: completed album sessions, client handoff folders, oral histories, field-recording dumps, sample-library source material, and safety copies.

Good fit

  • Large WAV trees that should stay recoverable for years.
  • Projects where metadata chunks and wrapper bytes matter.
  • Offline archives that need manifests and audit evidence.

Not the job

  • Active DAW sessions that need instant playback.
  • Lossy delivery copies or streaming transcodes.
  • A replacement for backups in multiple locations.

Lossless with receipts

Every Verified item has to survive a real restore.

01

Hash the original WAV

WavCrusher streams SHA-256 over the full source file, not only decoded samples.

02

Encode to a temporary archive

The pinned WavPack encoder writes a non-final .partial file with no overwrite behavior.

03

Decode in scratch space

The temporary .wv is restored with wvunpack, away from the source and final archive trees.

04

Compare whole-file SHA-256

Only a matching length and full-file hash allows final publication as a .wv archive.

Three practical workflows

Archive, audit, restore.

The archive is useful only if you can keep proving it. WavCrusher's version 1 design includes creation, later audit checks, and restore workflows that write clear reports instead of optimistic success banners.

  1. ArchiveScan a source root, preflight conflicts, encode, verify, publish, and journal results.
  2. AuditRecheck archive hashes and decoder integrity without modifying source or archive files.
  3. RestoreRecreate WAV files under a separate root and compare against archive-time hashes.

No private vault

Plain WavPack recovery stays available.

WavCrusher writes ordinary .wv files and documents the bundled tool identity. The app should make archiving easier, but it must not be required to get your WAVs back.

PS> .\wvunpack.exe "E:\Archive\Session\Mix.wv" "D:\Recovered\Session\Mix.wav"
PS> Get-FileHash "D:\Recovered\Session\Mix.wav" -Algorithm SHA256
